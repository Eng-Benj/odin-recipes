Pesto

Description

Pesto is a simple but highly effective pasta topping popular all across Italy. Traditionally made with a mortar and pestle, there are a wide variety of flavours. This page outlines the recipe for two of the most popular forms of pesto; Genovese and Calabrese.

Pesto Genovese

Ingredients

  • 25g of basil leaves
  • 50ml of extra virgin olive oil
  • 30g of Parmigiano Reggiano, grated
  • 10g of pine nuts
  • 1/3 of a clove of garlic
  • 1/3tsp of salt

Steps

  1. Add the garlic and salt to the mortar and crush the contents with the pestle.
  2. Add the pine nuts and 1tbsp of olive oil, then continue to crush until none of the pine nuts remain whole.
  3. Add the basil and the remainder of the olive oil, then crush and mix until the basil is reduced to a paste.
  4. Add the crated Parmigiano Reggiano and mix together well.

Pesto Calabrese

Ingredients

  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1/2 of a red chilli, chopped and seeded
  • 100g of sundried tomatoes in oil
  • 30g of Parmigiano Reggiano, grated
  • 1tbsp of extra virgin olive oil
  • 1tsp of oregano
  • 1/3tsp of salt

Steps

  1. Add the garlic, red chilli, salt, oregano and olive oil to the mortar and crush the contents with the pestle.
  2. Slowly add the sundried tomatoes (approximately 20g at a time) then crush and mix until it is reduced to a paste.
  3. Add the crated Parmigiano Reggiano and mix together well.
